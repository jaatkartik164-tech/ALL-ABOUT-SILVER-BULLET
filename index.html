Stop Loss Rule
Your stop loss is placed beyond the swept liquidity level â€” not inside the FVG, not at a random price. For a long trade, SL goes a few pips/ticks below the sweep low. TP1 â€” Minimum (1:1 R:R): The first structural high or low before the sweep. Close 25â€“50% of the position here. This guarantees the trade is not a full loss. Move SL to breakeven immediately after TP1 is hit.

TP2 â€” Standard (2:1 to 3:1 R:R): The opposing session's liquidity pool. For a long taken off the AM session low, TP2 is the AM session high or PDH. For a short, it's the SSL below. Let the runner reach here.

ICT Glossary â€” Silver Bullet Terms

FVG
Fair Value Gap. A 3-candle price inefficiency where candle 1's wick and candle 3's wick leave an unfilled gap. This imbalance represents a zone where price traded so quickly that not all buy/sell orders were matched. Price gravitates back to these zones before resuming the prior direction.
Liquidity Pool
A price area where a cluster of resting orders (primarily stop losses) are known to exist. Formed by obvious technical levels like equal highs/lows, swing points, previous session extremes, and round numbers. The draw for institutional order flow seeking to fill large positions.
BSL / SSL
Buy-Side Liquidity (resting stops above highs â€” sell stops trigger a buy surge) and Sell-Side Liquidity (resting stops below lows â€” buy stops trigger a sell surge). Smart money hunts BSL before bearish moves and SSL before bullish moves to fill their own institutional positions.
Liquidity Sweep
The act of price trading through a liquidity pool, triggering the resting stop orders there, then reversing sharply. The sweep is the first confirmation event in the Silver Bullet sequence. It can manifest as a pronounced wick or a brief series of candles that penetrate and immediately reverse from the liquidity level.
Kill Zone
A specific 60-minute window of elevated institutional price delivery activity. Silver Bullet kill zones: London Open (3â€“4 AM EST), NY AM (10â€“11 AM EST), NY PM (2â€“3 PM EST). Trading outside these windows using this model removes the core time-based edge.
Order Block (OB)
The final opposing candle before a significant institutional price delivery move. A bullish order block is the last bearish candle before a strong bullish impulse. Price often returns to order blocks as they represent zones where institutions placed large directional orders â€” making them high-probability support/resistance on the next retest.
PD Array
Premium/Discount Array. The ranked hierarchy of institutional price levels: Breaker Block > Mitigation Block > FVG > Order Block > Rejection Block > Void. Used to identify which zones carry the highest probability of price reaction. The Silver Bullet FVG entry is more powerful when it coincides with a higher-ranked PD Array level.
AMD Cycle
Accumulation â€“ Manipulation â€“ Distribution. The 3-phase daily delivery model. Accumulation: Asian range defines the initial order book. Manipulation: London/NY open raids liquidity in one direction (the "fake" move that traps retail traders). Distribution: Price delivers to its actual target in the opposing direction. The Silver Bullet targets the transition from Manipulation to Distribution.
PDH / PDL
Previous Day's High and Previous Day's Low. Primary liquidity reference points marked before every session. PDH carries buy-side liquidity (stops from short sellers and breakout buyers). PDL carries sell-side liquidity. These are among the first levels to check when mapping your Silver Bullet session setup.
Displacement
A strong, one-directional institutional price delivery move characterized by large-bodied candles with minimal wicks, creating one or more FVGs in its wake. Displacement after a liquidity sweep is the second confirmation in the Silver Bullet sequence â€” confirming that institutions have filled their orders and are now delivering price.
Inducement
A deceptive setup structure designed to trap premature entries. An FVG that forms before the liquidity sweep has occurred is inducement â€” it will likely be invalidated when the sweep happens. ICT teaches that if a setup looks almost too perfect before any sweep has occurred, it is probably inducement. Wait for the actual sweep first, always.
NWOG / NDOG
New Week Opening Gap / New Day Opening Gap. Price inefficiencies created between session closes and opens. These gaps represent unfilled orders and act as magnets for price delivery. A Silver Bullet setup near an NDOG has additional confluence because institutional delivery toward the gap aligns with the setup direction.
Dealing Range
The high-to-low range of a given reference period (daily, weekly, monthly). The upper half of the dealing range is considered premium (favorable for sells, unfavorable for new buys). The lower half is discount (favorable for buys, unfavorable for new sells). Silver Bullet longs should be taken in discount; shorts in premium.
Breaker Block
A failed order block â€” an order block that price has already traded through and broken. Once broken, the prior support becomes resistance and vice versa. Breaker blocks rank higher than standard order blocks in the PD Array hierarchy and represent zones where institutions have both stops and new directional orders resting.
