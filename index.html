Overview & Core Concept

The ICT Silver Bullet is a precision, time-based entry model developed by Michael J. Huddleston (ICT). It exploits the predictable manipulation that occurs during three specific 1-hour kill zone windows each trading day ‚Äî targeting a liquidity sweep followed by a Fair Value Gap entry.

Core Premise
The market is engineered to deceive retail traders. During specific hourly windows, smart money (market makers, institutions) raids liquidity pools (stop losses clustered above/below swing points), creates an inefficiency (FVG), then reverses. The Silver Bullet is designed to ride that reversal.
The 3 Ingredients
1. TIME ‚Äî You must be in the correct 1-hour window. Outside of it, no trade.
2. LIQUIDITY SWEEP ‚Äî Price raids a pool of stops (equal highs/lows, previous swing, overnight range).
3. FAIR VALUE GAP ‚Äî After the sweep, a 3-candle FVG forms in the opposing direction. You enter on retracement into this gap.

Setup Criteria ‚Äî Step by Step

Execute every step in order. Skip none.

1

Establish Daily / HTF Bias (Pre-Session)

Before the NY open, mark your daily chart bias. Are we in a bullish or bearish premium/discount array? Is price above or below the previous daily open? Identify the dealing range: previous day's high (PDH), previous day's low (PDL), and the weekly opening price. Your Silver Bullet must align with the HTF bias.

2

Mark Liquidity Pools Before the Window Opens

Identify where stop losses are clustered. Look for: equal highs / equal lows (double tops/bottoms), previous session swing highs and lows, overnight range highs and lows, any obvious chart structure where retail would place stops. Mark these with horizontal lines before the window opens.

3

Wait for the Window to Open ‚Äî Do Nothing Until the Sweep

When the clock hits :00, switch to a 1-minute or 2-minute chart. Watch price approach the marked liquidity pool. Do NOT anticipate the sweep. Do NOT enter early. You are waiting for price to break through the liquidity level ‚Äî a definitive wick or close beyond equal highs/lows or the swing point.

4

Confirm the Liquidity Sweep

The sweep is confirmed when price spikes through the liquidity pool with a strong wick or series of candles, then begins to reverse. Look for a large wick rejection candle or a quick spike-and-reverse. This sweep should happen within the window. The sweep doesn't have to close beyond the level ‚Äî a wick is sufficient. The key is that stop orders were triggered.

5

Identify the Silver Bullet FVG

After the sweep, look for the first FVG that forms on the opposing move. This is a 3-candle pattern where candle 1's wick and candle 3's wick do not overlap, leaving an "imbalance" or gap. This FVG must form AFTER the liquidity sweep ‚Äî not before it. Label this as your entry zone.

6

Enter on Retracement into the FVG

Set a limit order at the 50% midpoint of the FVG, or at the top/bottom edge of the gap (more conservative). You can also enter at market when price returns to the FVG zone. The trade is live the moment price enters the FVG. If price blows through the FVG without holding, the setup is invalidated ‚Äî do not chase.

Fair Value Gap ‚Äî Deep Dive

The FVG (also called an Imbalance or Inefficiency) is the engine of the Silver Bullet entry. Master this pattern precisely.

üìê
How to Draw the FVG

Mark from the low of candle 1 to the high of candle 3 (bullish) or high of candle 1 to the low of candle 3 (bearish). The gap must exist ‚Äî no overlap between C1 and C3 wicks.

üéØ
Entry Refinement

Enter at the 50% midpoint of the FVG for a balanced risk/reward, or at the near edge of the gap (more conservative, tighter SL). Some traders use the full FVG as the entry zone.

‚úÖ
Valid FVG Criteria

Must form after the liquidity sweep. Must be on the 1m or 2m chart within the window. The FVG gap must be measurable ‚Äî at least 2‚Äì3 pips / points wide. Larger FVGs = higher confidence.

‚ùå
Invalid FVG Situations

FVG forms before the sweep. FVG is too small (less than 1‚Äì2 pips). FVG is on a candle that has already been fully retraced. Multiple FVGs stacked ‚Äî take the one closest to the sweep.

Determining Market Bias

Your Silver Bullet trade direction must align with the higher timeframe narrative. Never trade a bearish Silver Bullet in a bullish HTF environment and vice versa ‚Äî unless you are trading a premium/discount reversal at a major institutional level.

‚Üë Bullish Bias Confirmation

  • Higher highs + higher lows on 4H/Daily
  • Price in a discount (below 50% of daily range)
  • Daily / weekly candle is bullish
  • AMD cycle: Price in accumulation or manipulation phase
  • NY open above Asian session midpoint
  • Price above breaker block / order block
  • News / macro context is bullish
‚Üì Bearish Bias Confirmation

  • Lower highs + lower lows on 4H/Daily
  • Price in a premium (above 50% of daily range)
  • Daily / weekly candle is bearish
  • AMD cycle: Price in distribution or manipulation phase
  • NY open below Asian session midpoint
  • Price below bearish order block / breaker
  • News / macro context is bearish
AMD Cycle Context
ICT's Accumulation‚ÄìManipulation‚ÄìDistribution model: In a bullish day, AM session often manipulates below the opening range to raid lows (stops), then distributes higher. In a bearish day, the inverse occurs. The Silver Bullet window typically catches the manipulation-to-distribution turning point.

Stop Loss & Take Profit Rules

Stop Loss Placement
Hard Rule: SL goes beyond the swing point that was raided (the liquidity pool). For a bullish trade, SL is placed a few pips/ticks below the swing low that was swept. For a bearish trade, SL is placed a few pips/ticks above the swing high that was swept. Add a small buffer (1‚Äì3 pips for Forex, 1‚Äì2 points for indices) to account for spread and slippage. Never put SL inside the FVG.
Take Profit Targets ‚Äî Hierarchy
Use this hierarchy for TP placement, from most conservative to most aggressive:

TP1 (Minimum ‚Äî 1:1 R:R): Previous structural high/low before the sweep. Close 25‚Äì50% here.
TP2 (Standard ‚Äî 2:1 to 3:1 R:R): Opposing session's liquidity pool (e.g. previous day's high for longs). Move SL to breakeven after TP1.
TP3 (Runner ‚Äî 4:1+): Major PD Array, weekly high/low, or the daily range extension. Trail SL behind structural highs/lows.
Breakeven Protocol
Once TP1 is hit and you close partial position (25‚Äì50%), immediately move stop loss to entry price + spread. This eliminates risk on the remaining position. Never let a winner become a loser after TP1.
Invalidation ‚Äî Close the Trade Immediately
If price re-enters and closes beyond the liquidity level that was swept ‚Äî the setup is invalidated. Do not hope. Close the trade manually at market. This signals that the sweep failed and a continuation in the sweep direction is occurring.

ICT Glossary ‚Äî Silver Bullet Terms

FVG
Fair Value Gap. A 3-candle price imbalance where the wick of candle 1 and the wick of candle 3 do not overlap, leaving an inefficiency in price. Price tends to return to this zone before continuing.
Liquidity Pool
A cluster of pending orders (stop losses) above a swing high (BSL ‚Äî buy-side liquidity) or below a swing low (SSL ‚Äî sell-side liquidity). Equal highs/lows are the most obvious liquidity pools.
BSL / SSL
Buy-Side Liquidity (above highs) and Sell-Side Liquidity (below lows). Smart money hunts these pools to fill their large orders before reversing direction.
Liquidity Sweep
The act of price trading through a liquidity pool (beyond equal highs/lows or a swing point), triggering the stop orders there, and then rapidly reversing direction.
Kill Zone
A specific time window with elevated institutional activity. Silver Bullet Kill Zones are: London (3‚Äì4 AM), NY AM (10‚Äì11 AM), NY PM (2‚Äì3 PM) EST.
Order Block (OB)
The last bearish candle before a bullish impulse (bullish OB) or the last bullish candle before a bearish impulse (bearish OB). Price often returns to these zones as support/resistance. Used as confluence with FVG entries.
PD Array
Premium / Discount Array. A set of institutional price levels: OB, FVG, Breaker Block, Mitigation Block, Rejection Block, Void. Price interacts predictably with these levels.
AMD Cycle
Accumulation ‚Äì Manipulation ‚Äì Distribution. The 3-phase daily price delivery cycle. Accumulation: Asian range builds orders. Manipulation: London/NY open raids liquidity (the "fake" move). Distribution: True directional move takes price to its target.
PDH / PDL
Previous Day's High and Previous Day's Low. Key liquidity levels that price frequently targets during Silver Bullet windows. Always marked before the session opens.
NWOG / NDOG
New Week Opening Gap / New Day Opening Gap. The gap between Friday's close and Sunday's open (NWOG) or between previous session close and current open (NDOG). Price often seeks to fill these gaps.
Displacement
A strong, one-directional price move with momentum that creates FVGs. The Silver Bullet entry displacement is the move that follows the liquidity sweep. Characterized by large candles with minimal wicks on the body.
Inducement
A setup that looks like the correct entry but is actually designed to trap retail traders. Usually occurs before the real sweep. If your FVG forms before the sweep, it may be inducement ‚Äî wait for the actual sweep first.
R:R
Risk-to-Reward ratio. Calculated as: (Distance to TP) √∑ (Distance to SL). A 2:1 R:R means your potential profit is twice your potential loss. Silver Bullet setups should never be taken below 2:1.
Mitigation Block
A failed order block that price trades through. After the break, if price returns to this level it often acts as strong resistance/support. Useful for confluence in Silver Bullet setups.
